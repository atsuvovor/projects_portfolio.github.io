Project Overview & Executive Summary

This interactive report details the analysis and improvement plan for critical issues identified within a new credit card sales process at a major financial institution. Implemented six months prior with objectives to enhance speed, ease, and client satisfaction, the process unfortunately led to declining Financial Advisor (FA) adoption, reduced credit card sales, and decreased client satisfaction. This application walks through the DMAIC (Define, Measure, Analyze, Improve, Control) methodology employed to address these challenges.

The goal is to provide a clear understanding of the problem, the data-driven analysis, the proposed solutions, and the expected outcomes, fostering a more efficient, user-friendly, and client-centric sales process. 1. Define the Problem

The Define phase is foundational, establishing the precise problems to be addressed, the overarching project goals, and the key metrics that will be used to track progress and success. For the credit card sales process, it became clear that initial performance post-launch was significantly below expectations, impacting both internal operations and the client experience.

Problem Statement:

The new credit card sales process, implemented six months ago to enhance efficiency and client satisfaction, is failing to meet its objectives. Specifically:

  • Declining Financial Advisor Adoption: The percentage of FAs utilizing the new process steadily decreased from an initial high of 85% to 60% within six months, indicating resistance or difficulty in using the new system.
  • Decreased Credit Card Sales: Overall credit card sales volume suffered a noticeable decline of approximately 33% (from an average of ~180 units/month before to ~120 units/month after) since the new process was introduced.
  • Lower Client Satisfaction: Recent client surveys revealed a drop in average client satisfaction scores from 8.0 (pre-implementation) to 6.5 (post-implementation) on a 1-10 scale, particularly concerning the credit card application experience.

Project Goal:

To identify and address the root causes of declining adoption, sales, and client satisfaction related to the new credit card sales process, ultimately leading to a more efficient, user-friendly, and client-centric process that drives increased sales and satisfaction, aligning with the bank's strategic objectives.

3. Analyze Root Causes

The Analyze phase involved in-depth investigation of the data collected in the Measure phase, specifically focusing on Financial Advisor interviews, client survey comments, and system logs to determine the true causes of the performance decline. The goal was to move beyond the symptoms and identify the fundamental issues.

Financial Advisor Feedback Analysis (Average Ratings: 1 - Poor to 5 - Excellent)

Ratings from the FAs indicated significant pain points across usability, speed, and support, which directly correlate to the decline in adoption and sales volume.

Identified Root Causes (Based on Data & Analysis)

Cause 1: Process Complexity & Redundancy

The new system requires FAs to enter the same client information multiple times across different screens (e.g., demographic, employment, financial), increasing cycle time and the risk of manual error. This makes the process less efficient than the old, simpler system.

Cause 2: Underwriter Delay for Exceptions

Applications that require underwriter review are subject to a long and inconsistent queue (avg. 2-3 days). This delay significantly harms client experience and disrupts the FA-client meeting flow, leading FAs to bypass the system to preserve client satisfaction.

Cause 3: System Performance and Glitches

The new system experiences frequent, intermittent slowdowns and minor data-saving glitches, forcing FAs to restart applications or re-enter data. This leads to advisor frustration and a perception of the system as unreliable.

Cause 4: Lack of Integrated Document Upload

When additional documents are requested, FAs must use a separate, clunky portal for submission, which is not directly linked to the application itself. This broken process contributes directly to the increased cycle time and manual rework.

4. Improve the Process

The Improve phase involved brainstorming, selecting, and implementing solutions to address the identified root causes. The primary focus was on simplifying the user interface, automating manual steps, and reducing process variation.

Proposed and Implemented Solutions:

Solution 1: Smart Form Integration (Addressing Cause 1)

Action: Implemented a single-page smart form interface with auto-population logic. When data is entered once, it is automatically mapped to all subsequent required fields in the system.

  • Eliminates redundant data entry for FAs.
  • Reduces the risk of manual data entry errors.
  • Expected to reduce the average Cycle Time by 8-10 minutes.
Solution 2: Automated Document Request & Upload (Addressing Cause 4)

Action: Integrated the application system with a secure client portal. The system now automatically emails the client a direct link to upload necessary documents when requested by an underwriter, eliminating FA manual follow-up and the separate portal.

  • Reduces FA rework and client chase-up time.
  • Documents are instantly tagged to the application file, improving underwriter efficiency.
Solution 3: Underwriter Triage Automation (Addressing Cause 2)

Action: Developed an automated triage system that prioritizes applications based on complexity and potential financial value, ensuring simple exceptions are reviewed within 4 hours. Dedicated "fast-track" reviewers were assigned to these simple cases.

  • Reduces the average Underwriter Wait Time for 60% of cases by 80%.
  • Improves client satisfaction by providing a quicker outcome for most non-instant approvals.

5. Control and Sustain the Gains

The final phase of DMAIC focuses on standardizing the improved process, establishing control measures to prevent regression, and creating a monitoring plan to ensure the long-term success of the project's gains.

Control Plan Highlights:

  • Process Standardization: Update all internal training manuals, FA workflow guides, and system documentation to reflect the new Smart Form and Automated Triage processes. Mandate a one-week training session for all FAs and Underwriters on the new system features.
  • Statistical Process Control (SPC): Implement automated system monitoring using a Control Chart for Process Cycle Time. Alert the Process Improvement team automatically if the average cycle time exceeds the upper control limit (UCL) of 35 minutes for three consecutive weeks.
  • System Audits & Maintenance: Schedule quarterly system performance audits and security reviews to proactively address glitches and slowdowns before they impact the FA experience.
  • Feedback Loop: Re-institute a monthly FA feedback survey (1-5 scale) and monitor results. Assign a dedicated IT support liaison to address FA pain points reported via the survey within 48 hours.

Success Criteria and Hand-off:

The project will be formally handed off to the Process Management team once the following criteria are sustained for three continuous months:

  • FA Adoption Rate: Consistently maintained above the target of 85%.
  • Monthly Sales Volume: Consistently achieved at or above 180 units/month.
  • Client Satisfaction: Consistently maintained above the target of 8.0/10.
  • Process Cycle Time: Consistently maintained below the target of 30 minutes, with the majority of exceptions (non-instant approvals) resolved within 48 hours.
  • FA Perceptions: Average satisfaction with the process (ease, speed) maintained above 4.0/5.

Conclusion:

The DMAIC project successfully identified and resolved critical root causes impacting the credit card sales process. The implemented solutions are projected to significantly improve efficiency, drive FA adoption, and enhance client satisfaction, ensuring the long-term success and competitiveness of the credit card sales channel.
